Five Step Population Proportion Hypothesis Test

Step 1: State The Hypothesis


The null hypothesis (\(H_0\)) is that the failure rate of the MPT is 10%. The alternate hypothesis (\(H_A\)) is that the failure rate is greater than 10%. We get the following:

\[ H_0 = .10\] \[ H_A > .10\]

Step 2: Identify the Test Statistic


The test statistic is the observed value that is being tested. In this case the test statistic is the proportion 18.4 %.

Step 3: Calculate the Rejection Region


\[ \mbox{SD} = \sqrt{\frac{pq}{n}} = \sqrt{\frac{0.1 *0.9}{449}} = 0.01415788\] \[ z = \frac{\hat{p}-p}{SD} = \frac{0.158-0.1}{0.01415788} = 4.096659\] \[ p-\mbox{value}= P(z>= 4.096659) = 0.000021\]

Five Step Population Proportion Hypothesis Test (Continued)

Step 4: State a Statistical Conclusion


Since the \(p\)-value (0.000021) is less than the alpha value (0.10) the null hypothesis is rejected.

Step 5: State the English Conclusion


Based on this sample there is significant enough evidence to suggest that the proportion of students who fail there first math class after scoring a 10 or better on the MPT is greater than 10%.

Five Step Permutation Test

Step 1: State The Hypothesis


The null hypothesis is that the mean GPA of students in STEM related majors who participated in the SOAR program is no different than the mean GPA of the students in STEM related majors who didn't participate in the SOAR program. The alternate hypothesis is that the students who participated in the SOAR program have a higher mean GPA than the students who did not participate in the SOAR program.

\[H_0: \mu_{\mbox{GPA SOAR}} - \mu_{\mbox{GPA nonSOAR}} = 0\] \[H_0: \mu_{\mbox{GPA SOAR}} - \mu_{\mbox{GPA nonSOAR}} > 0\]

Step 2: Identify the Test Statistic


The test statistic is the observed value that is being tested. In this case the test statistic is the difference of 0.508.

Five Step Permutation Test (Continued)

Step 3: Calculate the Rejection Region


Using the permutation test we can see that we have a p-value of 0.0584942.

Step 4: State a Statistical Conclusion


Since the p-value (0.0584942) is less than the alpha value (0.10) the null hypothesis is rejected.

Step 5: State the English Conclusion


Based on this sample there is significant enough evidence to suggest that the students who are STEM majors and attend the SOAR program have a higher GPA than students who are STEM majors and do not participate in the SOAR program.
